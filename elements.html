What instrument would truly reflect the waves that an instrument should have?

Instruments generating music- can happen in many different ways. An instrument creates music/sound by causing something to vibrate. For a wind instrument that might be the column of air. For the string instrument, it would be the strings, and for the drum, it it the stretched skin. However, for electrical music instruments, this would be reflected in a different manner. Unlike acoustic instruments, these electrical music do not make actual sounds as a acoustic instrument would. Most electric instruments produce an electrical signal, in which would be transmitted to an amplifier, and broadcasted using a loudspeaker. They can make sounds like acoustic instruments through a process called synthesis.

How are waves affecting my instruments?

    Music can be produced in many ways. Since different instruments use different methods to produces sound waves, they have classified the instruments into 5 main groups (originally 4, but later adding the 5th due to having electronic musical instruments)

    Here are the groups:

    Idiophones- this is where the entire instrument will vibrate, in which include xylophones, cymbals, maracas. Membranophones- these produce sound by vibrating the membranes, these can include kazoos, and drums. Chordophones- where the vibrating strings cause the sound waves. These include violins, and guitars or any stringed instrument. (standing wave) Aerophones- (standing waves in pipes) when the air is vibrating to create the wave. (any flute, reed, or long tubed instrument) Electro-phones- Produce vibrations by electronic means.

Citation with the text quote

Bash the Trash. "How Instruments Work - In Depth." How Instruments Work - In Depth. Bash the Trash, 2013. Web. 05 Mar. 2016.

"The Sachs-Hornbostel system classified instrument in four main groups (later adding a fifth with the introduction of electronic instruments such as the theremin). •Idiophones – where the entire instrument vibrates (such as a xylophone bar, maracas, cymbals, etc.) •Membranophones – which produce sound by vibrating membranes (drums, kazoos) •Chordophones – vibrating strings •Aerophones – where air vibrates - horns, flutes, reeds, and some others •Electrophones – vibrations produced by electronic means "

Notes

How do theremin’s produce waves?

Theremin’s produce waves through having an electrical circuit in which is connected to a speaker. The speaker amplifies the wave to create music. An electric instrument produces an electrical circuit, in which is amplified by the speaker. This does not work in the same way as a normal instrument, as it does not run on the vibrations of the instrument itself, but on the electrical circuit.

How does an electrical circuit make waves? How are waves produced?

The electrical circuit itself does not produce any sound but produces a current of energy, and that is amplified by the speaker.

Are they digital or analog signals?

Analog signal, because we have an oscillator and don’t have a digital circuit. We don’t have a digital circuit to create a digital waves.

WHat changes the signal’s amplitude?

The potentiometer which is connected to the circuit helps control the signal, as it is attached directly to the speaker. By adjusting the potentiometer (in which adjusts the volume of the speaker) will adjust the signal’s amplitude. The more energy inputted, or allowed into the speaker, the more volume is outputted.

What is pitch?

The pitch changes with the frequency of the wave. The more frequent the wave, the higher the pitch. Since my theremin is light-sensitive, and is a photo theremin
