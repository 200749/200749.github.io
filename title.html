555 IC Timer

What does the 555 IC timer do?

It is the oscillator for the theremin. The 555 timer can be used in a variety of different ways such as timer, pulse generation, and in this case I used it as an oscillator. An oscillator is used for generating electric currents, and voltage, by non-mechanical means.

CDs Photocells

Did I manage to buy it?

Yes

What does the CDs Photocell do?

Photocells are sensors for light, and allow you to detect light. As they are exposed to more light, the resistance goes down. When the light intensity changes, it changes the oscillators frequency, producing a higher or lower sound.

What does the 5k potentiometer do?

For my theremin it helps:

    The potentiometer changes the amplitude of the speaker. Also, it helps change the amplitude of the light. The potentiometer helps adjust the voltage power of the light or sound coming through the source. Since the amplitude and energy have a ratio relationship, the more voltage, the more amplitude.

      In addition

      These were the materials I used that were in addition to the ones that were needed for the original theremin. This allowed me to better control my theremin, and produce steadier and louder tones.

      Change-Larger speaker

      Why did I change the speaker?

      I changed to a larger speaker, so that there would be more vibrations. This helped produce a larger sound, and also helped with the cymatic production of the instrument.

      Change-LED light schematic

      What was this design?

      In this design I managed to add more control to my theremin by adding a row of LED lights. By doing this I could have LED lights next to the photocells (which control allow the light to control the frequency produced) I then put the switches for these LED lights outside of the box so that I could control the LED lights, and control the sound produced. I also changed the design by adding the light that I could control, which involved more schematics, and LED lightbulbs. I also changed the design so that it was in the box instead, so that I could control almost all the light going to the theremin.

      Change-Aluminium foil

      Why did I add aluminium foil?

      I added aluminium foil to help produce more reflection (in which it is not specular reflection but scattered) and this helped the light produce more intensity, because it was reflecting off the surface, instead of being absorbed.
